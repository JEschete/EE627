	\section{Problem 1: True/False Justification}
	%======================================================================

	\textbf{(a) Statement:} ``For a stationary AR(1) time series $x(t)$, $x(t)$ is uncorrelated to $x(t-l)$ for $l\geq 2$.''

	\textbf{Answer: FALSE.} For AR(1), the theoretical autocorrelation is
	\[
	\rho(l)=\phi^l,\qquad l\geq 0.
	\]
	In general, this is nonzero for $l\geq 2$ when $\phi\neq 0$, so the values are not uncorrelated.

	\vspace{0.75em}
	\textbf{(b) Statement:} ``For a stationary MA(1) time series $x(t)$, you will observe the ACF values cliff after time lag $l\geq 2$ in the ACF plot.''

	\textbf{Answer: TRUE.} For MA(1), the ACF cuts off after lag 1, so for $l\geq2$ the theoretical autocorrelation is 0.
